The Story

We mine luxury.
We don't live it.

Opal is one of the most beautiful things that comes out of the ground anywhere in the world. And then it leaves. It goes into someone else's ring, someone else's collection, someone else's life.

That's the job. We go in, we find it, and it goes. We wouldn't have it any other way — but it's worth saying out loud, because most people never think about the hands it passed through to get there.

Opal mining in Andamooka and Mooka hasn't changed much in decades. Small crews. Hard ground. You read the dirt, you follow what the earth gives you, and some days it gives you nothing. You dig anyway.

Once a stone leaves the claim, where it came from is forgotten. It becomes part of the trade. That's always been the deal.

We want to change that — slowly, carefully, from the inside.

"We pull some of the most beautiful stones in the world out of the ground. Then they go to someone else. That's the job."
Who We Are

Matt & Cozza Kathagen

Brothers. Miners. We've been at this our whole lives. You might know us from Outback Opal Hunters, but out here in outback South Australia that's not what we're known for. We're known for showing up — to the claim, to the community, to the work.

This isn't a phase. It's our life. There's no glamour at the bottom of a shaft. No comfort in the dirt. Just the two of us, our gear, and whatever the ground decides to give us that day.

We've given up a lot to keep doing this. Comfort, certainty, the kind of life most people take for granted.

We know what it is to go without. We'd choose it again tomorrow.

We go into the ground to keep our family above it.

What we need right now is people who want to be part of it. Not spectators. Crew.

— Matt & Cozza
